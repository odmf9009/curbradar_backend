Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 26, 2026

CurbRadar ("we", "our", or "us") is operated by Venturesfl Studio. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your information when you use our mobile application.

1. Information We Collect

  • Account information: When you register, we collect your name and email address (via email/password or Google Sign-In).
  • Location data: We collect your device's approximate and precise location to show nearby curbside items and to pin items you post on the map. Location is only accessed while using the app.
  • Photos and images: When you post an item, you may upload photos from your device. These images are stored securely on Firebase Storage.
  • Usage data: We may collect information about how you interact with the app (screens visited, items posted or claimed) to improve the experience.
  • Device information: We collect basic device identifiers and operating system information for analytics and crash reporting.

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To create and manage your account
  • To display nearby free curbside items on the map
  • To allow you to post, claim, and browse items
  • To send you push notifications about new items in your area (only if you grant permission)
  • To improve the app through analytics
  • To respond to support requests

3. Third-Party Services

CurbRadar uses the following third-party services, each governed by their own privacy policies:

  • Firebase (Google LLC) – authentication, database, storage, and push notifications. Firebase Privacy Policy
  • Google Maps – map display and geocoding. Google Privacy Policy
  • Google Sign-In – optional social login.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties. We only share data with third-party services listed above as necessary to operate the app.

5. Data Retention

Your account data is retained as long as your account is active. You may request deletion of your account and associated data at any time by contacting us.

6. Children's Privacy

CurbRadar is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us so we can delete it.

7. Your Rights

Depending on your location, you may have rights to access, correct, or delete your personal data. To exercise these rights, contact us at the email below.

8. Security

We use industry-standard security measures, including encrypted data transmission (HTTPS) and Firebase security rules, to protect your information.

9. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify you of significant changes by updating the date at the top of this page.
